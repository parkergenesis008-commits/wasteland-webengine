## Phenomenon

Engineered quantum semiconductor heterostructures (quantum wells) with magnetically doped layers and subband-engineered bandgaps act as resonant cavities for axion-photon conversion via the **inverse Primakoff effect** ($\mathcal{L}_{a\gamma} = g_{a\gamma} a \mathbf{E} \cdot \mathbf{B}$). When an axion passes through the heterostructure in an external magnetic field, it converts into a detectable photon. The resonant cavity enhances conversion probability by matching the axion mass to the quantum well subband transition energy: $\omega_{cav} = E_{gap} + E_{subband}$.

The conversion cross-section scales as $\sigma_{a \rightarrow \gamma} \propto g^2_{a\gamma} B^2 Q_{cav} V_{cav}$, where $Q_{cav}$ is the cavity quality factor from the heterostructure design. Target mass range: 1–10 meV.
